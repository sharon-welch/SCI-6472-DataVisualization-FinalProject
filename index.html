Women's Health and the Bike Seat: A proposal for new saddle design

Reasons for large existing gender disparities in biking are not fully understood. In the US, only 2.2% of trips are taken by bike. Furthermore, only .6% of that percentage are taken by women.

In looking at national data on daily commutes, only 27% of trips are made by women.

Furthermore, women's health and comfort when it comes to bicycling are understudied. For example, saddle sores are an issue often reported by cyclists. A review of literature conducted in 2022 showed that only 2/15 of the most prevalent studies on saddle sores focused on women.

While existing research (cite this) has shown that women feel less safe while biking and are generally less willing to bike in areas without cycling infrastructure, there has been very little research done to explore whether design of the bike itself influences female ridership. Here, we explore existing factors that influence health of cyclists to search for potential correlations between health, bike seat design, and ridership disparities. This exploration is based on the a hunch that saddle design does in fact influence ridership.
